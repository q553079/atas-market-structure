# Liquidity Provider Retreat Under Toxicity

## Status

- `status`: `human_refined`
- `source_type`: `inventory-risk and adverse-selection doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This card separates fake depth from rational retreat.

Sometimes passive size disappears because it was bait.
Sometimes it disappears because the environment became too toxic to keep defending the same price.

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- whether a level was previously defended with real outcome
- whether one-way aggressive flow became unusually toxic or one-sided
- whether passive size retreated, repriced, or defended deeper instead of staying
- whether the retreat happened before touch or under real pressure
- whether a new accepted level formed after the retreat

## Confirmation Signals

- a previously real defender stops standing at the old price
- one-way pressure or toxicity rises while passive quotes retreat or reprice
- the market begins referencing a new defended area instead of the old one

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not assume static DOM size will stay when toxicity rises
- do not label every retreat as spoofing without branch context
- do not keep leaning on the old defended price after the provider clearly repriced

## Management Notes

- this card is strongest when prior defense was real and later changes state
- retreat and reprice can still leave a new level worth following
- hand off to real support or resistance logic once the new reference price is proven

## Review Questions

- was the provider baiting or rationally retreating under pressure
- did the operator keep trusting the old price after the defense already moved
- where did the new defended reference actually appear
