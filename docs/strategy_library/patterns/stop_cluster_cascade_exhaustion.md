# Stop-Cluster Cascade Exhaustion

## Status

- `status`: `doctrine_accepted`
- `source_type`: `stop-cascade and acceptance doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This card describes what happens after a stop cluster is triggered.

The cascade itself can be powerful.
What matters next is whether the cascade becomes accepted trade or only an exhaustion burst.

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- where the stop cluster likely sat
- how violent the release was once triggered
- whether price held outside after the cascade
- whether the first pullback was defended or not
- whether same-side initiative continued after the stop fuel should have been mostly spent

## Confirmation Signals

- the cascade releases quickly once the stop area is breached
- outside trade either stays accepted or loses hold quality on the first meaningful retest
- the post-cascade branch becomes clearer than the initial burst

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not chase the first stop cascade before outside acceptance is visible
- do not fade the cascade immediately before outside hold quality actually fails
- do not assume that a violent extension automatically created new accepted value

## Management Notes

- stop fuel can create distance faster than true initiative can create structure
- the best continuation usually comes after the first retest holds
- if the cascade cannot rebuild after release, the move often slips into repair

## Review Questions

- was the operator trading stop fuel or accepted continuation
- after the cascade, did outside trade hold or fail
- where did the market prove exhaustion or prove rebuild
