# NQ Measured Move Ladder Context

## Status

- `status`: `human_refined`

## Pattern Summary

This card describes measured-move logic as a ladder, not a single target.

The useful version of measured move requires:

- a real impulsive leg
- body-confirmed progress through thresholds
- continuity of the leg despite small internal pauses
- disciplined refusal to upgrade targets from wick noise alone

Measured move is valid when the market is still carrying the same leg.
It becomes weak when the market is already overlapping, balancing, or forcing the operator to measure random fragments.

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- the leg being measured is continuous enough to count as one campaign
- threshold clears are based on body progress, not random wicks only
- the move can be tracked through first, second, and later target stages
- pullbacks inside the leg are small enough to preserve continuity
- later stall or failure is recorded at the threshold where it actually happened

## Confirmation Signals

- the leg remains structurally continuous while price progresses through thresholds
- body closes confirm the next stage before the target ladder is upgraded
- price reaction near each threshold is orderly enough to decide whether the move is still expanding or beginning to stall

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not measure random wicks, overlapping chop, or unrelated fragments as one clean leg
- do not upgrade the target ladder before body confirmation appears
- do not force measured-move logic when the broader script has already shifted into balance or reversal

## Management Notes

- keep the current active threshold explicit
- if the next ladder stage fails to confirm, reduce ambition rather than inventing a larger target
- the measured move should support context and management, not replace location and reaction

## Review Questions

- was the operator measuring the real leg or just reacting to screen noise
- what body-confirmed threshold actually justified target escalation
- did the market keep the same leg alive, or had it already transitioned into a different script
