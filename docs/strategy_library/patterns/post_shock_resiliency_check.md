# Post-Shock Resiliency Check

## Status

- `status`: `doctrine_accepted`
- `source_type`: `microstructure resiliency doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This card separates the shock itself from what happens after the shock.

A burst, sweep, stop run, or harvest can look decisive.
The better question is:

- did the book rebuild for continuation
- or did the market start repairing back toward balance

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- what kind of shock happened first
- whether spread and depth stayed dislocated or normalized quickly
- whether the first pullback held or failed
- whether gained ground was defended after the initial burst
- whether continuation survived after the emotional phase ended

## Derived Interpretation

These belong in `derived interpretation`:

- `post_shock_resiliency`
- `spread_depth_rebuilt`
- `shock_repair_not_continuation`
- `first_pullback_hold_quality`
- `rebuilt_for_continuation`

## Confirmation Signals

- the first shock completes and the next pullback becomes readable
- price either holds the gained ground and re-accelerates or loses it and repairs
- post-shock behavior becomes cleaner than the shock itself

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not chase the first shock before the first pullback is visible
- do not auto-fade a shock only because spread widened during the event
- do not call the first pause a failure without checking whether the new ground is still defended

## Management Notes

- the first pullback is usually the best diagnostic checkpoint
- if gained ground cannot hold after the shock, downgrade the continuation read quickly
- if depth and trade continue rebuilding under the new side, continuation quality improves

## Review Questions

- did the operator trade the shock instead of the post-shock branch
- after the burst, did the market rebuild or repair
- where exactly did continuation stop being obvious or start becoming obvious
