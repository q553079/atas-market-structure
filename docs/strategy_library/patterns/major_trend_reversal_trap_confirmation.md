# Major Trend Reversal Trap Confirmation

## Status

- `status`: `doctrine_accepted`
- `source_type`: `price-action trap reversal doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This pattern formalizes the higher-value version of a major trend reversal: a breakout side first looks right, then becomes trapped, and only after confirmation does the reversal become tradeable.

The key business distinction is:

- a market being "high" or "low" is not enough
- one failed bar is not enough
- a trap matters when breakout traders lose the exact level they needed to hold, and their exits become fuel for the reversal

This is the confirmation-focused version of MTR logic, not the impatient version.

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- which trend or leg was active before the reversal setup
- where the breakout side first looked strong
- which bar or sequence trapped the breakout side
- which level objectively proved those traders wrong
- whether the market closed through that level with body confirmation
- whether the first pullback after confirmation failed to rescue the trapped side

## Confirmation Signals

- the breakout side first gets enticed into continuation
- price then closes beyond the level that objectively invalidates those followers
- the first rescue attempt fails and the opposite direction gains travel

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not front-run a reversal before trap confirmation exists
- do not use this logic when the setup is still only a normal pullback in a strong trend
- do not trust the pattern if the signal side was weak and never truly trapped committed traders

## Management Notes

- confirmation is more important than calling the exact top or bottom
- the best follow-through often comes after the first failed rescue by the trapped side
- once the trapped-side release is largely spent, late chasing becomes lower quality and can hand off into balance

## Review Questions

- who exactly got trapped, and where did they become objectively wrong
- did the reversal thesis come from real confirmation or from fading an old trend emotionally
- after confirmation, did the trapped side get a rescue chance and still fail
