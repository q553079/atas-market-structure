# Control Handoff After Harvest

## Status

- `status`: `human_refined`
- `source_type`: `post-objective control doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This pattern describes what happens after one side completes a visible objective and the market begins deciding whether control stays with that side or transfers to the other side.

The key business question is:

- was the objective completion the end of the campaign
- or only a waypoint before the same side continues

This card is not about the harvest itself.
It is about the first evidence that control may be changing hands after the harvest.

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- which side completed the objective
- how much same-side continuation happened after completion
- whether the first post-harvest pullback was shallow or structurally damaging
- whether the opposite side could establish acceptance away from the harvested area
- whether the completed side could defend on first revisit
- whether the market shifted into rotation, reversal, or renewed continuation

## Derived Interpretation

These belong in `derived interpretation`:

- `control_handoff_under_review`

- `temporary_pause_not_handoff`

## Confirmation Signals

- same-side continuation weakens soon after the objective is completed
- the opposite side achieves real acceptance on the first meaningful counter move
- the original winning side cannot defend the first important revisit after harvest

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not force a control handoff read just because the market pauses after objective completion
- do not call control transferred before the opposite side can hold acceptance away from the harvested area
- do not keep pressing old-direction continuation if the old winner already failed the first meaningful defense

## Management Notes

- this card is strongest when paired with post-harvest response rather than used alone
- the first failed defense after harvest matters more than random noise between two small pullbacks
- once control truly hands off, old continuation logic should be downgraded quickly

## Review Questions

- what proved that control actually changed hands after the harvest
- did the old winning side fail its first real defense, or only pause
- was the operator still trading the completed campaign after the market had already changed script
