# Forced Exit Burst Vs Initiative Continuation

## Status

- `status`: `doctrine_accepted`
- `source_type`: `inventory release and control-takeover doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This pattern separates a move driven mainly by forced exits from a move driven by fresh initiative.

The confusion usually looks like this:

- one side gets trapped or loses a key defense
- exits produce a fast vertical burst
- the burst looks like strong continuation
- but the new side never proves durable control after the old side is done exiting

This matters because not every squeeze, flush, or release is a trend.
Sometimes price only reprices the trapped inventory and then falls back into repair or balance.

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- where the trapped or uncomfortable inventory came from
- whether the burst followed a failed reclaim, failed defense, or harvest completion
- whether the move was mostly one fast release or continued with fresh same-side effort
- whether gained ground was defended on the first meaningful pullback
- whether new initiative kept pressing after forced exits should have been mostly completed
- whether the move quickly widened into balance once the burst ended

## Derived Interpretation

These belong in `derived interpretation`:

- `forced_exit_burst`

- `long_liquidation_not_new_selling`

- `exit_fuel_exhausted`

## Confirmation Signals

- the burst begins after a clear trap, failure, or forced unwind becomes active
- the first pullback after the burst shows whether new control exists
- continuation only becomes credible if the gaining side can hold and re-accelerate after the exit fuel fades

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not chase a vertical burst before testing whether it is mostly forced exit fuel
- do not assume new initiative exists if the gained ground is not defended on the first meaningful retest
- do not fade the burst only because it is vertical if the new side is actually holding and rebuilding

## Management Notes

- the best continuation read usually comes after the first shallow pullback holds
- if post-burst balance widens quickly, the move was often more exit fuel than true control transfer
- once the exit fuel is spent, target logic should shift from emotional release to accepted auction structure

## Review Questions

- was the operator chasing trapped-side exits and calling them fresh initiative
- did the new side defend gained ground after the burst, or only enjoy the release
- when the burst slowed down, did the market continue with control or slip into repair and balance
