# Gap Bar Retracement Continuation

## Status

- `status`: `doctrine_accepted`
- `source_type`: `price-action continuation doctrine synthesis`

## Pattern Summary

This pattern describes a clean displacement leg that creates a meaningful imbalance, followed by the first pullback into the reaction zone that continuation traders are expected to defend.

The core idea is not to chase the impulse bar itself.
The core idea is to wait for the market to test whether that impulse truly changed the auction.

A valid Gap Bar in this doctrine can be:

- one very strong trend bar
- a small sequence of nearly non-overlapping trend bars
- an implicit imbalance zone where one side moved price so quickly that the first return matters more than the first burst

## Observed Facts To Preserve

- where the displacement leg started and ended
- whether the move away from balance was clean or noisy
- whether the first pullback was shallow, orderly, or already too deep
- whether the pullback reached a meaningful reaction band instead of random middle noise
- whether the first retest held and led to renewed travel in the original direction
- whether the pullback turned into acceptance back inside prior balance

## Confirmation Signals

- the initial impulse clearly leaves the prior balance or pullback structure
- the first retracement holds in the expected reaction band instead of collapsing through it
- continuation resumes with a clean reclaim, defended pivot, or renewed directional travel

## No-Trade Conditions

- do not call overlapping chop a Gap Bar
- do not buy or sell a late recycled retest as if it were the first pullback
- do not use continuation logic if the pullback is already accepting back into prior balance

## Management Notes

- the first pullback is the business test; later retests are lower quality
- if the retracement becomes too deep, downgrade continuation and consider balance recovery instead
- if continuation resumes but quickly stalls, reassess whether the original displacement was real or only emotional

## Review Questions

- was the impulse truly clean enough to justify first-pullback continuation logic
- was this really the first meaningful retracement, or a late retest after the move had already degraded
- did the operator enter in the reaction zone, or chase after the edge was already gone
